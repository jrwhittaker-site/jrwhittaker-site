An excerpt from Title of Script — a short film written and directed by JR Whittaker.

Title of Script

INT. LOCATION — DAY A spare room. Late afternoon light cuts across the floor in long parallels. Dust moves slowly through it, unhurried. A chair sits at the center — occupied, though it takes a moment to be sure. CHARACTER ONE (40s, still in the way that comes from practice) stares at something just off the far wall. Their hands rest open on their knees. A knock. Then silence. INT. HALLWAY — CONTINUOUS The door at the end of the hallway is slightly ajar. Light from the room bleeds underneath it. CHARACTER TWO stands with one hand raised, mid-knock, as if they've forgotten what comes next. Character Two (quietly) I wasn't sure you'd still be here. A long beat. The door opens another inch on its own — no draft, no explanation. Just open. Character One I wasn't sure either. Character Two steps through. The hallway light dies behind them. Neither of them looks at the door. INT. ROOM — CONTINUOUS The light has shifted. It always does. Character Two finds the corner of the room and stays there, taking in the space as if measuring it for something. Character Two How long has it been like this? Character One (a pause) I stopped counting when it stopped mattering. Character Two nods. Slowly. As if this is the answer they came for. The dust moves through the light. The chair remains. FADE TO:
