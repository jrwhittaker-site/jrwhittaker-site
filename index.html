Mentor & Educator

The work of asking the right questions.

For eighteen years I taught and chaired the film department at the New York Film Academy, building curriculum deployed across twenty countries. The methodology was always the same: lead with questions, not answers. Help the filmmaker find the story only they can tell.

That same approach applies whether I am working with a director on set, a producer in development, or a student preparing their portfolio for conservatory or college admissions.

If you are a student applying to a performing arts high school or a college film program, I work with you the same way I work with any filmmaker. We find your story. We make sure every choice is in service to it. Sessions are available virtually and in person in New York.

About

Still asking questions.

I came to filmmaking the way most do: by watching too many films and asking too many questions. Twenty years later the questions have not stopped. I have directed for Sony Pictures, the BBC, Gillette, Hyundai, and Nissan. I have produced for Netflix and Amazon. I have taught and mentored filmmakers across five continents. And I am still making short films and writing screenplays in Brooklyn because the work keeps teaching me things I do not yet know. Based in New York. Available worldwide.

Even a small kiss of light is enough to ignite a spark.
